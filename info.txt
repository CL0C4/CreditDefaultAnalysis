-- INTERPRETING RESULTS
tn: true negatives, good borrowers that the model identified correctly
tp: true positives, high-risk borrowers that the model identified correctly
fp: false positives, good borrowers that the model identified incorrectly
fn: false negatives, high-risk borrwers that the model identified incorrectly

recall: sensitivity to default, i.e. of all the people in the dataset that actually defaulted, how many were successfully identified by the model(as a percentage), want this to be as high as possible
precision: of all the borrowers that the model predicted would default, how many actually did
roc-auc score: Area Under the ROC curve, the model's overall ability to sort borrowers from high-risk to low-risk, a higher value means a better sorting power
f1-score: balanced average between precision and recall, i.e because the dataset has far more non-defaulters than defaulters, it could just say no one would default and be highly accurate, the f1-score forces the model to balance catching defaults without hurting precision
